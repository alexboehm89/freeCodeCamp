John Higgins - The Wizard of Wishaw

John Higgins, one of the best Snooker players of all time, after winning one of his four World Championships.

Early years

  • 1992: Turning professional at the age of 17
  • 1995: Winning 3 ranking titles, the Grand Prix, the British Open, and the International Open
  • 1996: Becoming number 2 in the world of snooker.
  • 1998: World Champion at 23 years old and becoming number 1 of the world.

After the first world title

  • 1998: Higgins won the UK Championship and the Master, becoming the third player to hold all Triple Crown titles simultaneously.
  • 1999: He was replaced as the number one, despite winning the UK championship and the Grand Prix.
  • 2000: As the first player ever, Higgins won the three opening tournaments of a season.
  • 2005: Higgins scored 4 consecutive centuries in the Grand Prix final.
  • 2006: In a final against Ronnie O'Sullivan, Higgins won his second Masters.

Second and third world titles

  • 2007: Higgins won his second World Championship in a final against Mark Selby. He regained his number one position in the World Rankings.
  • 2008: Fourth title at the Grand Prix.
  • 2009: Higgins could win his third World Championship with a 18-9 win against Shaun Murphy.
  • With a victory at the Welsh Open, Higgins could remain the world number one.

Match-fixing allegations and fourth world title

  • 2010: Suspension due to match-fixing allegations. Allegiations were later dropped.
  • 2010: Higgins won the UK Championship for the third time.
  • 2011: Another title at the Welsh Open for John Higgins.
  • 2011: After beating Ronnie O'Sullivan, Mark Williams, and Judd Trump, John Higgins won his fourth World Championship.

Struggle with form

  • 2012: During the 2011/2012 season Higgins only reached two quarter finals.
  • 2013: After winning his 25th ranking title at the Shanghai Open, he struggled for the rest of the year and did not win another tournament.
  • 2014: During the 2013/2014 season Higgins changed his cue frequently, but he never found his form and didn't win any major tournaments.
  • 2015: After almost dropping out of the Top 16 in the world during the 2014/2015 season, Higgins improved his form despite not winning any titles.

Return to form

  • 2016: Higgins returned to his good form and could win a fourth Welsh Open title. He reached the finals of the World Championship but lost.
  • 2017: In 2017 Higgins played well all year long and could win multiple titles. Again, he reached the finals in the World Championship, but lost.
  • 2018: Higgins could reach another World championship final, but lost again, for the third year in a row.
  • 2019: In this year, Higgins played the 800th century of his career, surpassing the great Stephen Hendry.

First ranking title in three years

  • 2021: After multiple years without a title, Higgins could win the Players Championship, after "the best week of [his] snooker career".
