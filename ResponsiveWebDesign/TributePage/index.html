John Higgins - The Wizard of Wishaw

John Higgins, one of the best Snooker players of all time, after winning one of his four World Championships.

Early years

  • 1992: Turning professional at the age of 17
  • 1994 - 1995: Winning 3 ranking titles, the Grand Prix, the British Open, and the International Open
  • 1995 - 1996: Becoming number 2 in the world of snooker.
  • 1998: World Champion at 23 years old and becoming number 1 of the world.

After the first world title

  • 1998 - 1999: Higgins won the UK Championship and the Master, becoming the third player to hold all Triple Crown titles simultaneously.
  • 1999 - 2000: He was replaced as the number one, despite winning the UK championship and the Grand Prix.
  • 2000 - 2001: As the first player ever, Higgins won the three opening tournaments of a season.
  • 2005: Higgins scored 4 consecutive centuries in the Grand Prix final.
  • 2006: In a final against Ronnie O'Sullivan, Higgins won his second Masters.

Second and third world titles

  • 2007: Higgins won his second World Championship in a final against Mark Selby. He regained his number one position in the World Rankings.
  • 2008: Fourth title at the Grand Prix.
  • 2009: Higgins could win his third World Championship with a 18-9 win against Shaun Murphy.
  • With a victory at the Welsh Open, Higgins could remain the world number one.
